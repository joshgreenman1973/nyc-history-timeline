Sources & method

How this timeline was built, and where every date comes from.

Approach

This is a curated selection of turning points in the history of New York City — not a comprehensive chronicle. Events were chosen to span the full arc of the city, from the Lenape homeland of Lenapehoking through Dutch New Amsterdam, English colonial rule, the Revolution, the 19th-century boom, the 1898 consolidation of the five boroughs, and the modern metropolis.

Each event was checked against reputable references. Every card carries a direct link to a source — encyclopedic, governmental, archival or scholarly — so you can verify the claim yourself. Dates that are approximate or contested are marked c. and flagged with a "medium" confidence label.

Editorial notes

Descriptions aim to state facts without editorializing. Where a date is traditionally given but debated by historians, the traditional date is used and noted. This project favors clarity over completeness: many worthy moments were left out to keep the story legible.
